Ai & the Future of Productivity

Projects that once took months, will soon take hours...


The Future of AI: What OpenAI Just Exposed

Why AI could reshape work & society

Opening / Big Idea

AI is evolving rapidly - what once took decades or generations could soon take minutes/hours

We may be approaching a major societal "reset"

The coming decade could bring more disruption than the last 150 years

The Core Concept: "Overhang & Spikiness"

Overhang: AI's potential far outpaces how much we currently use it

Spikiness: Once adoption catches up, change will happen very fast - not gradually

Combined → many tasks/jobs become suddenly automatable

Which Jobs Are at Risk / What Could Change

Jobs heavy on routine, predictable, repetitive tasks:

Administrative / clerical work

Data entry, reporting, standard analysis

Basic coding, simple debugging / maintenance

Email drafting, documentation, workflows

These may become "AI-able" soon

What Adapters & Early Adopters Gain

If you embrace AI early and build workflows around it, you can:

Dramatically boost productivity (do more with less)

Free up time for higher-value, creative, strategic work

Get "leverage" - machine scale + human judgment

What You Should Do Now (as Individual / Worker / Engineer)

Audit your tasks: which are machine-readable (code / data / text)?

Delegate low-risk, repetitive tasks to AI first (docs, tests, analysis, boilerplate)

Focus on building skills: orchestration, automation, AI-human integration

Think about long-term: your value may shift from "doing" to "designing & supervising"

Why This Is More Than "Better Tools" - It's a Paradigm Shift

It changes what "value" means (speed, scalability, automation)

Could disrupt job stability, conventional career paths, education norms

Traditional institutions (org hierarchies, long-term employment) may need to adapt

What's Uncertain / What the Risks Are

Displacement: people whose jobs are mostly repetitive may struggle

Inequality: benefit may favor early adopters or highly skilled workers

Social challenges: safety nets, retraining, adapting workforce may lag

Why This Resonates for Data / Knowledge Workers (like Engineers)

Your work (data pipelines, ontology, automation) aligns well with machine-readable workflows

You're positioned to gain much from AI - automation plus human judgement

Demand may shift toward roles combining domain knowledge + AI orchestration

Call to Action: What to Do Next

Experiment now: pick a small routine task and automate it with AI

Learn AI-oriented tooling / practices (tests, prompt-engineering, automation)

Think strategically: what workflows / roles will be most "AI-resistant / AI-leveraged"?

Stay adaptable - invest in skills that won't be easily replaced

Summary / Key Takeaways

AI isn't just incremental - we may be near a major shift

Jobs & work as we know may transform rapidly

Early adopters who combine human + AI work stand to benefit the most

For many, success requires rethinking what "work" means and embracing automation

Discussion / Questions

What parts of your current work are most automatable?

How could AI change your team's workflow in 1–3 years?

What skills will matter most in the AI-augmented future?
